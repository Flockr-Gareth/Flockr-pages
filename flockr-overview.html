1 · The product

What Flockr does

Flockr is a real-time demand intelligence + content & social proof platform. It turns live behavioural signal into targeted on-site messages and content — activated at the exact moment a decision is being made, across every surface in the journey. E-commerce is the first and most commercially mature application; the underlying architecture is category-agnostic.

Signal

Flockr analyses user behaviour in real time: product & content views, user-journey, add-to-carts, purchases, search activity, stock movements, and momentum patterns. It classifies products & content by lifecycle state, ranks by demand velocity, and detects scarcity before it becomes a problem.

Selection

A 13-step pipeline evaluates every eligible product & content against 11 signal families and chooses the right message & content for each surface. A per-client adaptive scoring model learns which signals convert best for each site and re-weights accordingly.

Activation

Messages & content render across on-site surfaces — Home, forms, PDP, PLP, search, cart, checkout, recommendations, delivery, membership. Every impression is attributed through a six-tier exposure model with dose-response evidence of causal lift.

The full loop — from user action to message & content rendered to outcome attributed — runs in milliseconds. No creative brief, no campaign setup, no manual tagging. The system sees demand forming, selects the right response, and delivers it before the decision is made. Catalogues of products & content are the current application; the same architecture fits any domain where discrete items compete for limited attention.

How we know the lift is real

Every attributed conversion is assigned to an exposure tier based on how much of the Flockr experience a shopper saw during their session. The tiers form a dose-response curve: shoppers exposed to more Flockr messages convert at progressively higher rates than shoppers who saw none. That graduated response — not a single before-and-after number — is what separates causal lift from correlation, and it is why the figures published above are defensible to a finance team.

4 · Defensibility

What makes it defensible

Messaging widgets are a commodity. A real-time signal engine, an adaptive scoring model that improves per-client with every session, and a conversational AI layer that lets operators interrogate the system in natural language — together, that is not.

Adaptive scoring

The scoring model re-weights per client based on what actually converts for their shoppers/users. A fashion retailer and a home-improvement store end up with very different signal emphasis — automatically, with no manual tuning. In production across the client base.

Closed-loop AI development

Flockr’s entire engineering workflow runs on a governed AI operating model: two Claude surfaces with separate authority, bridged by committed files, gated by prompts that refuse to proceed if documentation and code are out of sync. The codebase compounds rather than degrades.

Signal — conversational intelligence

Signal is the AI assistant embedded in every page of the Flockr portal. It knows how Flockr works, the client’s specific configuration, and their live store data — all three at once. Operators ask questions in plain language and get source-attributed answers in seconds.

Architecture beyond e-commerce

The signal-selection-activation architecture is not catalogue-specific. The core pattern applies wherever there are items to surface and decisions to influence. A media property can run it on articles, with read-completion and scroll-depth as signals and feed positions as surfaces. A marketplace can run it on listings, ranking by demand velocity and freshness. A SaaS product can run it on features, surfacing the right one to the right user at the right moment in onboarding.

The scoring formula, slot allocation model, exposure-tier attribution methodology, and governance approach all transfer without modification. The signal taxonomy and surface definitions are the variables; the system architecture is the invariant. E-commerce is the commercially mature first application — not the ceiling.
